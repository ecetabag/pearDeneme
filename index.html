What is Pear?

Pear is a platform created to enable middle and high school students to collaborate with other students. They can ask their help for a quiz, exam, homework or anything they need help with. By simply filling out the appointment form, the student gets matched with a mentor. The meeting time and information is sent to their mails. The meetings are done online, via Google Meets, which enables the students and the mentors to come together easily. Fill out the form to arrange a meeting with a mentor down below.

Mentors

Mentors consist of high school students who are passionate about helping other students. The mentors are selected carefully. Teacher recommendations, school grades, and social activities are considered in the admissions process. We believe that teaching a peer a subject helps to understand it better; therefore, it is beneficial for both the mentors and the students. The mentors are matched with a new student every session, allowing mentees to learn from different perspectives. Mentors will receive a certificate after they complete a certain number of tutoring sessions. To become a mentor, you can fill out the "Mentor Application Form."
